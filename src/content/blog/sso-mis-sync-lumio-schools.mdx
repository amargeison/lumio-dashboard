## Schools Are Bleeding Money on Middleware

Here's a number nobody talks about: the average UK school spends between £3,000 and £8,000 per year on MIS middleware, data connectors, and integration tools — just to make their existing software talk to each other.

That's not a technology investment. That's a tax on bad architecture.

Your MIS doesn't talk to your safeguarding platform. Your safeguarding platform doesn't talk to your attendance system. Your attendance system definitely doesn't talk to your SEND provision tracker. So you buy Clever, ClassLink, Wonde, or GroupCall connectors to bridge the gaps. Each one costs money. Each one adds another vendor to manage. Each one introduces another point of failure.

And the worst part? Most of these middleware providers are US companies that don't understand Ofsted, don't support UK SEND requirements, and couldn't tell you what KCSIE stands for.

## The Problem Is Structural

Legacy school MIS platforms — SIMS, Bromcom, even Arbor — were built as closed systems. They were designed to be the centre of your school's data universe, not one node in a connected ecosystem.

So when you need to connect a new safeguarding tool, or pull pupil data into your curriculum planning system, you're looking at expensive API integrations, middleware subscriptions, or (the most common approach) a member of staff manually exporting a CSV and re-importing it into another system every Monday morning.

That's 2–3 hours per week of skilled staff time spent on data entry that a machine should handle. Multiply that by 40 weeks and you're looking at 80–120 hours of lost productivity per year. Per school.
