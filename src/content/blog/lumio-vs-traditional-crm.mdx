## The CRM Problem

Let's be honest: most CRM software is terrible.

Not terrible in the way that it doesn't work — Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive all technically function. They're terrible in the way that they make your sales team's life harder instead of easier. They demand hours of manual data entry. They generate reports that nobody reads. They cost a fortune in per-seat licensing that scales painfully as your team grows.

And the worst part? They exist in complete isolation from the rest of your business. Your CRM knows everything about your pipeline but nothing about your delivery capacity, your support ticket backlog, or whether your operations team can actually fulfil what your sales team is promising.

For enterprise companies with dedicated CRM administrators and six-figure software budgets, this is manageable. For growing UK businesses with 10 to 200 employees, it's a disaster.

## What's Wrong with Salesforce and HubSpot for SMBs

**Salesforce** is the industry standard, and it earned that position. But it was built for enterprises with complex, multi-layered sales organisations. For a UK business with a sales team of 3-15 people, Salesforce is like using a crane to hang a picture frame. The implementation takes months. The customisation requires a consultant. The per-user pricing starts at £20/month and quickly climbs to £120+ when you need the features that actually matter — and that's before you add the integration costs.

**HubSpot** positioned itself as the Salesforce alternative, and its free tier is genuinely useful for getting started. But the moment you need marketing automation, custom reporting, or more than the basic pipeline view, you're looking at £700-£3,000 per month. The pricing tiers are designed to get you hooked on the free version and then extract maximum revenue as you scale.

**Pipedrive** is simpler, which is both its strength and its limitation. It does pipeline management well, but it's purely a sales tool. It doesn't connect to your HR system, your project management, or your operations workflow. It's another silo.

### ARIA Intelligence

Every contact and deal in Lumio CRM is scored by ARIA, our AI engine. But unlike traditional lead scoring that uses crude point systems, ARIA analyses genuine engagement signals. It tracks email open velocity, LinkedIn activity changes, company funding rounds, hiring patterns, and dozens of other data points to give you a genuine picture of deal health.

When ARIA tells you a deal is at risk, it's not because someone forgot to log a call. It's because the champion's LinkedIn shows they're "Open to new roles" and the company just posted a hiring freeze announcement.

UK B2B sales are relationship-driven. Deals close over coffee, not through automated email sequences. Lumio CRM is built for this reality. The activity feed prioritises genuine interactions over automated touchpoints. The pipeline view shows deal momentum, not just stage position. The contact enrichment pulls from UK-specific data sources including Companies House, LinkedIn, and industry directories.
