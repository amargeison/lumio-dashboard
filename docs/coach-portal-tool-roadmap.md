# Lumio Coach — Tool Roadmap (deep dive)

*Written from a working coach's perspective, grounded in 2026 market research and mapped to the Lumio brand universe and partnership targets. Prioritised by impact vs effort.*

---

## The lens

A peripatetic coach like Pete spends his day in three modes: **on court coaching**, **running a business** (bookings, money, parents), and **developing players** over months and years. Research is blunt about where the pain is:

- The biggest time sink is admin — scheduling, court conflicts, and parent comms. Platforms that combine AI with rule-based automation save coaches roughly **8–10 hours a week**.
- **No-shows** are a silent revenue leak. Payment-at-booking plus automated reminders keeps no-show rates below ~10–15%, and SMS/email reminders alone cut them by ~**15%**.
- **Parent engagement drives retention** — parents who can see goals, homework and progress (ideally in an app, not a clunky portal) stay longer.
- Coaches consistently report **limited insight into player progress** and **poor lead tracking** as the things that lose them students.

What Lumio Coach already nails: lesson summaries (with AI brief, PDF, email/WhatsApp share), session planner, rich player development, the belt system (LTA-aligned, certificates), training camps, court planner, roster + welcome packs, resource centre, and messaging. That's a strong **coaching-content** spine. The gaps are mostly in **business automation**, **video/data**, and **the family-facing side**.

---

## Tier 1 — Win back the coach's time (highest ROI, build first)

These are the features the market says save 8–10 hrs/week and protect revenue. They're also the least glamorous, which is exactly why they win loyalty.

**1. Client-facing online booking & self-serve scheduling**
The Booking Calendar today is coach-side only. Add a public booking link/page where players and parents see open slots, book, and pay upfront — calendar updates automatically, no back-and-forth. This is the single most-requested operational feature in 2026 and the front door to everything else.
→ *Lumio tie-in:* powers the parent/player portal (Tier 3).

**2. Automated reminders, confirmations & follow-ups**
Booking confirmations, 24h lesson reminders, "credit running low" alerts, and post-session follow-ups — set once, run automatically via SMS/WhatsApp/email. Directly cuts no-shows ~15%. You already have the Send Message engine; this makes it *scheduled and rule-based*.

**3. Invoicing & online payments**
Payments & Packages tracks credits but doesn't *take money*. Add real invoicing + card/Direct Debit (Stripe-style), term/block billing, auto-receipts, and outstanding-balance chasing. Faster cash flow, fewer awkward "you owe me" conversations.

**4. Attendance register + makeup lessons**
A one-tap register for group sessions (present/late/no-show), automatic no-show flagging into the player's record, and makeup-credit handling. Closes the loop between "who turned up" and the attendance % already shown on player cards.

**5. Enquiry → trial → member CRM (lead pipeline)**
Capture website/WhatsApp enquiries, book trials, track conversion, and automate follow-ups. "Poor lead tracking = lost students" is a recurring research finding, and it's pure growth.

---

## Tier 2 — Coach better (differentiation, strong Lumio-brand fit)

This is where Lumio's owned-tech brand becomes a genuine edge versus generic booking software.

**6. Lumio Vision — video analysis** *(flagship)*
Record on court, then draw angles/lines, slow-mo, and place two clips **side-by-side** (before/after, or vs a model). Add AI skeleton/joint-angle tracking — 2026 tools do this from a phone camera, tracking 33 body points at 30fps and 100+ swing metrics, no sensors needed. This is the #1 *coaching-content* feature competitors (CoachNow) win on, it's brand-native (Lumio Vision), and it turns the Resource Centre's "video preview coming soon" into a real owned library.

**7. Match & tournament log + ratings**
Log competitive matches (opponent, score, surface, notes), maintain a tournament calendar with entries, and feed results into the belt system. Surface LTA/WTN/UTR-style ratings alongside belts so the development picture is complete.
→ *Lumio tie-in:* **Lumio Scout** for opponent prep.

**8. Lumio GPS — movement & load management**
Wearable/GPS movement data, training-load monitoring (acute:chronic workload), and recovery flags — exactly the JOHAN Sports partnership territory. Even a simple manual load tracker (session RPE × minutes) is valuable for performance juniors and injury prevention.
→ *Lumio tie-in:* **Lumio GPS** + JOHAN Sports partnership.

**9. Physical & athletic development**
Fitness benchmarks (speed, agility, endurance), periodic testing, and — important for juniors — growth/maturation tracking (height/weight, relative-age awareness). Helps justify *why* a player is at a belt and plan long-term.

**10. Player readiness / wellness check-ins**
A 20-second pre-session check (sleep, energy, soreness, mood) that trends over time. Cheap to build, surprisingly powerful for performance players and for spotting burnout or drop-off early (it'd have flagged Hannah Berg before her attendance dipped).

**11. Drill builder with court diagrams**
A visual session/drill designer — drag players, balls, cones and feeds onto a court, save to the Resource Centre, attach to a session plan. Makes the Session Planner tangible and builds your owned drill library.

---

## Tier 3 — Engage the family (retention engine)

Research is emphatic: parent engagement is the retention lever.

**12. Parent & player portal/app**
The mirror of the coach portal: families see schedule, homework, progress, belts and certificates, pay invoices, and book lessons. You already have the demo-workspace concept and the welcome pack — this closes the loop and is what keeps people paying month after month.

**13. Goals & player journal**
SMART goals per player, plus a simple player reflection/journal that parents can see. Ties the belt system and lesson summaries into a story the family is invested in.

---

## Tier 4 — Run a safe, compliant academy (UK trust layer)

Unglamorous but, for a coach working with juniors in the UK, table stakes — and a real trust signal for parents.

**14. Safeguarding & compliance hub**
DBS, first-aid and safeguarding-training expiry reminders; welfare officer details; codes of conduct and supervision ratios; **photo and medical consent collected at onboarding** (extend the welcome-pack questionnaire into a stored digital consent); and an incident/injury log. LTA Accreditation effectively requires this, and it's reassuring to parents.

---

## Tier 5 — Grow the business (analytics & scale)

**15. Academy analytics dashboard**
Utilisation, revenue trends, retention/churn, lifetime value, peak times, and coach hours. The "data-driven decisions" pillar — and the thing that tells Pete which programmes actually make money.
→ *Lumio tie-in:* **Lumio Data**.

**16. Multi-coach / team management**
When Pete adds assistant coaches: shared availability, session assignment, hours and payroll, and shared player notes.

**17. Integrations hub**
ClubSpark/LTA, Stripe, Google Calendar, wearables (JOHAN/Whoop/Garmin), and video hardware. Less to build, more to connect.

---

## Recommended sequence

1. **Quick wins next:** Attendance register (4), Automated reminders (2), Readiness check-ins (10) — small, high-value, build on what's already there.
2. **The differentiator:** Lumio Vision video analysis (6) — the feature that makes this *Lumio*, not generic booking software.
3. **The money:** Online booking (1) + Invoicing/payments (3) — the operational backbone parents expect.
4. **The moat:** Parent/player portal (12) — turns one-off lessons into a sticky subscription.
5. **The trust + scale layer:** Safeguarding hub (14) and Analytics (15) as the academy grows.
